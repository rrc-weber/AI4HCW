How Language AI Reads & Writes

Language models don't understand words the way we do. They chop text into tokens, turn each token into numbers, and then predict the next token one at a time. Walk through all three steps below — and meet the dial that controls how "creative" the writing gets.

Step 1

Tokenise — chop the text into pieces

First the text is split into tokens: common words become one token; longer or rarer words get split into sub-word pieces. Pick a phrase:

💡 Notice rare words like breathlessness break into sub-word tokens (breath ▸ less ▸ ness). This lets the model handle any word using a fixed vocabulary of pieces.
Step 2

Embed — turn each token into numbers

Every token becomes a list of numbers — a vector — that captures its meaning. Tokens with similar meaning get similar numbers, so they sit close together in this "meaning space". Tap any word to see its numbers and where it lives.

The model never sees letters — only these numbers. "Closeness" of numbers = closeness of meaning.
Step 3

Attention — decide which words matter

Before predicting the next word, the model looks back over the whole sentence and decides which earlier words to pay attention to. Relevant words get more weight; filler words get less. This is the breakthrough that makes modern LLMs work.

Step 4

Predict the next token — with a temperature dial

To write, the model looks at the text so far and produces a probability for every possible next token. Temperature reshapes those probabilities: low = always pick the safest, most likely token (predictable); high = give riskier tokens a chance (creative, sometimes odd).

What you just saw

✂️
Tokens, not words. Text is broken into pieces from a fixed vocabulary, so the model can handle anything — including words it has never seen.
🔢
Meaning becomes numbers. Each token is a vector; similar meanings sit close together. This is how NLP "understands" — by position in number-space, not by definition.
🔦
Attention picks what matters. Before each prediction the model weighs every earlier word and focuses on the relevant ones — that's the breakthrough behind modern LLMs.
🎲
Writing = predicting the next token, repeatedly. The model never plans a sentence; it just keeps choosing a likely next token. Temperature controls how adventurous those choices are.
⚠️
This is why it can "hallucinate". A fluent, confident sentence is just high-probability tokens strung together — there's no fact-checking step. Always review clinical output.
