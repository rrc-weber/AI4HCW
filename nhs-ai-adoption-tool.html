This tool supports NHS organisations in systematically assessing, governing, and adopting AI and digital health technologies across the full adoption lifecycle. It is intended for multidisciplinary teams including clinical safety officers, IG leads, clinical leads, IT, R&D, and project managers.

How to use this tool
1
Identify stakeholders
Use the Stakeholders tab to name your leads for each role before starting
2
Work through phases
Use the Assessment checklist tab — complete phases 1–10 in order, ticking items as you go
3
Track documents
Use the Documentation tracker to monitor which inputs are received and outputs are produced
4
Check local limits
Use the Local implementation tab to assess constraints specific to your organisation
The 10 adoption phases
1
Problem identification
Define the clinical problem and confirm AI/digital is the right solution
2
Evidence assessment
NICE evaluation, Evidence Standards Framework (ESF), DTAC review
3
Medical device regulation
UKCA/CE marking, MHRA registration, IFU, DCB0129 compliance
4
Clinical risk management — DCB0160
Legally required: appoint CSO, establish CRMS, complete clinical safety case
5
Data governance & DPIA
UK GDPR, Caldicott Principles, DPIA, data sharing agreements
6
Equality & bias assessment
Public Sector Equality Duty, algorithmic bias review, equality impact assessment
7
Local validation
Performance testing, stress testing, calibration, integration testing
8
Pilot study
Structured real-world testing using DECIDE-AI and NICE RWE framework
9
Training & go-live
CQC, IRMER, staff training, implementation plan, escalation pathways
10
Post-deployment monitoring
Drift monitoring, MHRA Yellow Card reporting, change management
